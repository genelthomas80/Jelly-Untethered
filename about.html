About Me

A little background on the person behind Jelly Untethered.

Hi, I’m Genel. Most people call me Jelly. I’m 45 years old and spent the majority of my life in Massachusetts. I’m single. Divorced single. Not “it’s complicated” single. Not “we’re friends but also sleeping together” single. Just… single. Honest. Possibly too honest, depending who you ask.

For most of my adult life, I did exactly what I was supposed to do. I went to college, earned a degree, paid off my loans, got married, built a career, bought a house, adopted many pets, earned a graduate degree, and advanced certifications in my field. I showed up. I stayed loyal. I played responsible human very well.

It worked… until it didn’t.

First came the divorce. Then unexpected changes at work. Then the quiet, uncomfortable realization that my life was getting smaller instead of bigger. Burnout showed up uninvited. Restlessness followed. And the question “Is this really it?” refused to leave. Life began to feel like a series of repeated motions. I was running hard, constantly in motion, but not actually going anywhere.

So instead of buying another couch, pretending a short vacation would magically fix everything, or picking up a questionable coping mechanism, I made a bigger decision.

In January 2026, I resigned from my job after twenty years. Twenty years in the same business. Twenty years helping grow it into something successful. That job was my identity, and I was proud of what I helped build. I still am. But I also see now that my career should never have been that much of who I was. There is more to life than just work.

I had no idea what was next. No job. No income. Naturally, I did the most logical thing a woman in her mid-40s could do: I booked a vacation to “think.”

I logged into my Holland America account, curious if I had any inexpensive cruise offers. Turns out, I had many. One week became two. Two became a month. A month became three. Before I knew it, I had a six-month itinerary mapped out. I ran the numbers. Rent. Utilities. Groceries. Snow plowing. And realized something wild: it would actually be less expensive to live on a cruise ship than to maintain my apartment.

So I gave notice to my landlord and decided to travel the world.

Not to escape my life, but to reconnect with it. To see who I am without job titles, routines, and expectations that no longer fit.

This site isn’t about perfect travel photos or pretending every day is magical. It’s about the real version of choosing yourself. The beautiful parts. The awkward parts. The lonely moments. And the surprising joy that shows up when you least expect it.

If you’re here because you’re curious, restless, stuck, or quietly wondering if you’re allowed to want more, welcome. And if you’re my friends or family, thank you for supporting this slightly unhinged but deeply intentional idea. Please follow me on one of my social pages as those will be updated more frequently than this website!

Thanks for being here. ❤️
